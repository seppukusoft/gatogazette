EXCLUSIVE: Leaked Files Show Sweeping Charges Against Previous Administration, DOJ and SDIOA Collusion

GG Investigations April 17, 2025
Note (4:50 AM EDT): The article was updated to correct the identity of the Attorney General when Notcom's charges were first dropped.

The Gato Gazette has been provided exclusive copies of multiple documents related to previous and future arrests and charges, including against former President Muggy, former OHS Director Notcom, and more.

Additionally, we have been provided screenshots of conversations between AG Benbookworm, SDIOA employees, and other government officials. These screenshots show that despite being aware of SDIOA's limited purview regarding charges (i.e. recommending but not pursuing), its employees are (possibly illegally) working with the DOJ to pursue charges on multiple officials.

These efforts include massive, simultaneous arrests (as was seen yesterday with Notcom and Myth, which are planned to continue) and using SDIOA employees' rights as private citizens to bring criminal cases that the previous AG declined to pursue.

The process of preparing for this moment goes back at least two weeks when SDIOA began investigating Operation Shanghai, a previously unknown OHS operation to gain intelligence on TIDE members through the use of double agents. SDIOA Investigator Terak completed the EX 01/2025 report on April 8th, but the report was not released until late on the 15th, a full week later.

The delay of its release was strange, but not necessarily suspicious‚Äîthat is, until the Gazette learned of the reason behind it.

The SDIOA released its report at 10:15 pm EDT, which concerned the OHS and SDAF's work on Operation Shanghai, but former OHS Director Mythrows, former OHS Director Notcom, and Secretary of Defense Strix were all arrested between 9:16 and 9:57 pm, before the report recommending charges against them was released.

Since arrest results in an immediate mute, the public has no basis for understanding why so many people were arrested in such a short time, and those arrested have no opportunity to explain themselves to the public or defend themselves in the court of public opinion.

This was not a coincidence, however, and we now have proof to back that up.

The above message details AG Ben's plan to investigate those involved in Operation Shanghai and charge them, including:

  • arresting them before any public reports or criminal complaints
  • fully muting the arrestees ("without holding cell perms")
  • charging them simultaneously
  • emptying the docket to move the charges to trial as quickly as possible
  • collaborating with SDIOA to "help" an investigation

Now, there are many revelations in this message from the two days before the plan went into motion; first, President Mypen is named here rather than Strix for reasons unclear, and AG Ben is specifically compelling SDIOA to investigate the president.

Next, Mythrows is named as someone to be charged in relation to Shanghai, but Myth was eventually charged on allegations of hate speech that most citizens have concluded was satirical. It is also unclear why Myth was charged in this way; it is entirely possible that since the SDIOA report, and subsequent DOJ investigation, revealed nothing worthy of criminal charges from them, they chose to pursue the hate speech allegations while still treating them the same as members alleged to have committed treason or obstruction of justice.

Additionally, although Myth's warrant has been publicly released (which is how citizens have been able to see the allegedly hateful comments), the warrants for Notcom and Strix's arrests have not.

The Gazette has also learned that the warrant for Myth's earlier arrest for "Revealing State Secrets," which started the New Vegas server drama, was both filed and signed by AG Ben. Typically, a warrant is filed by the DOJ to be signed by a separate judge, with the DOJ attorney recusing themselves if they are a judge; only in cases where the AG is also a judge and all other judges decline or recuse themselves is this allowed to happen.

So, although it is legal, the fact that all judges chose not to sign the warrant except the one who filed it in the first place could indicate that the other judges either had conflicts of interest (which AG Ben clearly had as well) or did not believe in the merits of the evidence. However, a review of relevant statutes by the Gazette has found no evidence of this being true, and a legal expert we contacted confirmed that the AG's interpretation is incorrect.

MBH's personal contribution was to draft a complaint to privately charge Notcom under Article 48 of the Criminal Code, "Revealing State Secrets."
